
For each simulation setting, latent signals $\Theta_1,\ldots,\Theta_n \in \mathbb{S}^2$ are generated independently from a multimodal spherical prior with a prescribed number of modes. We consider configurations with $1,\ldots,4$ modes.
paired respectively with concentration parameters $\kappa = 50,100,150,200$. The sample size is fixed at $n = 5000$ throughout.

Conditional on $\Theta_i$, noisy observations $X_i \in \mathbb{S}^2$ are generated according to an isotropic Riemannian normal distribution,
$
X_i \sim \mathcal{N}_{\mathbb{S}^2}(\Theta_i, \sigma^2),
$
where $\sigma^2$ denotes the noise variance. We examine increasing noise levels,
$
\sigma^2 = 0.01,\,0.025,\,0.05,\,0.1,\,0.25.
$


The proposed denoiser produces estimates with truncation level $M = 3,5,7,9$, 
with $M$ matched to the number of modes in the latent distribution, and fixed regularization parameter $\rho = 10^{-5}$.


For each combination of number of modes and noise variance, we perform $N_{\mathrm{MC}} = 10$ independent Monte Carlo replicates. Results are summarised via boxplots of relative improvement as a function of $\sigma^2$ and mean square loss curves for both naïve and denoised estimators, with respective losses given by
$
\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{N}}
= \frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^n d^2(X_i, \Theta_i),
$the denoised loss
$
\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{D}}
= \frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^n d^2\bigl(\delta(X_i), \Theta_i\bigr),
$
where $d(\cdot,\cdot)$ denotes the geodesic distance on $\mathbb{S}^2$.
